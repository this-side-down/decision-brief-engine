# Decision Brief

## Summary

Assign Marcus to the hospital project after a defined multifamily handoff. Promote Carlos on the school project only with Safety/Training sign-off. Open an HR superintendent backstop before the monthly staffing review and Thursday client call. Medium confidence until hospital start date and Carlos training status are confirmed.

## Decision Context

Portfolio workforce allocation under Q4 schedule pressure. The hospital project needs an experienced senior superintendent. The school project faces penalties without strong field leadership. Multifamily closeout is holding Marcus longer than planned. HR cannot fill gaps immediately. Promotion, travel, and subcontract options each carry safety, cost, and retention tradeoffs.

## Options Considered

- Reassign senior superintendent Marcus to the hospital project once multifamily closeout can transition
- Promote foreman Carlos to assistant superintendent on the school project with a defined training plan
- Hire a traveling superintendent or contract superintendent for the hospital start
- Subcontract self-perform concrete scope temporarily on the lower-priority warehouse job
- Delay or de-prioritize lower-risk warehouse work
- Split superintendent coverage across hospital and school critical paths temporarily

## Recommendation

Assign Marcus to the hospital project with an explicit multifamily handoff plan. Promote Carlos to assistant superintendent on the school project only with Safety/Training sign-off. Activate an HR backstop hire for superintendent coverage. Delay warehouse work or subcontract concrete scope if float cannot be protected.

## Risks and Constraints

### Risks

- Overloading senior superintendents such as Marcus
- Safety and compliance gaps if promotion happens before training is complete
- Client escalation on hospital or school projects
- Schedule penalties if experienced field leadership is pulled
- Attrition from travel fatigue and repeated reassignments
- Weak supervision on lower-priority projects during reallocation

### Constraints

- Hospital project start is approaching with a client call requiring a named superintendent
- School schedule has minimal float on key milestones
- HR candidates cannot start immediately and onboarding is slow
- Self-perform concrete crew is already overcommitted
- Decision is needed before the monthly staffing review and Thursday client call

## Open Questions

- What is the confirmed hospital project start date from the owner?
- When can HR candidates realistically start and at what cost?
- What is Carlos's certification and training status for school and potential hospital support?
- What is the real schedule float on the school project?
- Will clients tolerate a change in named superintendent coverage?
- Can multifamily closeout transition to PM-led coverage without quality risk?

## Suggested Next Steps

- Finalize a Q4 staffing matrix before the monthly staffing review
- Confirm schedule float with project controls on school and hospital work
- Decide promotion and foreman backfill plan for Carlos with named owners
- Validate safety and training readiness before any promotion or reassignment
- Assign HR owner for traveling or contract superintendent hiring path
- Revisit allocation decisions at the next workforce planning meeting

## Confidence

Confidence: Medium. Hospital start date, Carlos training readiness, and HR candidate timing remain unconfirmed.
