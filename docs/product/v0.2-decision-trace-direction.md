# DBE v0.2 Direction: Decision Trace

## Summary

Decision Brief Engine v0.1.0 made the system's understanding auditable through the Capture Layer. v0.2 planning focuses on making the judgment step auditable as well.

The proposed direction is:

Raw Input → Capture Layer → Decision Trace → Decision Brief

Decision Trace is a structured, user-facing rationale artifact that binds each recommendation and next step to the captured intent, evidence, assumptions, risks, constraints, tradeoffs, alternatives, and missing context it depends on.

Decision Trace is not raw model thinking, hidden reasoning, scratchpad output, or chain-of-thought.

## Problem

The Capture Layer shows what the system understood. The Decision Brief currently presents recommendations and suggested next steps, but those judgments are not yet traceably linked to the Capture Layer.

A reviewer needs to see why a recommendation follows from the captured context, what it depends on, how confident it is, and what would change it.

## Minimal conceptual model

Use:

Raw Input → Capture Layer → Decision Trace → Decision Brief

Do not use:

- a graph-based rationale map
- a scoring matrix
- raw model thinking
- a broad AI assistant pattern

Decision Trace should be a thin artifact between the existing Capture Layer and Decision Brief.

## Decision Trace artifact

A future Decision Trace entry should exist for each recommendation and next step.

Each entry should include:

- statement
- kind: recommendation or next_step
- intent served
- supporting evidence
- assumptions relied on
- risks addressed
- risks accepted
- constraints respected
- tradeoffs
- alternatives considered and why not
- missing-context caveats
- per-item confidence
- would_change_if conditions

## UI direction

Keep the existing three-column layout:

- Input
- Capture Layer
- Decision Brief

Do not add a fourth column.

In the Decision Brief column, each recommendation and next step should eventually have a collapsed Basis disclosure.

Expanded Basis should show:

- what the item is based on
- per-item confidence
- what would change the recommendation

## Export direction

Markdown export should eventually include the Decision Trace so exported briefs remain self-contained.

A brief that loses its rationale on export defeats the purpose of the trace.

## Eval direction

Future eval gates should verify:

- every recommendation has a trace entry
- every next step has a trace entry
- every trace entry has a non-empty basis
- every trace entry has per-item confidence
- every trace entry has at least one would_change_if condition
- basis items are grounded in the Capture Layer where practical
- generic change conditions are treated as weak or failing

## What must not change

- Mock-first public demo remains the default.
- Local Ollama remains the strongest real-generation path.
- Browser WebGPU remains gated.
- Hosted inference remains out of scope.
- Raw model thinking, hidden reasoning, scratchpad output, and chain-of-thought remain unexposed.
- The product does not become a broad AI assistant.
- v0.2 should avoid fundamental re-architecture.
